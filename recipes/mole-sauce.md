# Mole Sauce

Deeply flavored mole sauce built on toasted chiles, charred vegetables, warm spices, and dark chocolate.

## Ingredients
* 3 dried ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
* 2 dried guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
* 3 Roma tomatoes, halved
* 1 white onion, quartered
* 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
* 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
* 1 ounce dark chocolate (70%+), chopped
* 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
* 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
* 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
* 1/4 teaspoon MSG
* 1 tablespoon white miso paste
* 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
* 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

## Directions
1. Heat a dry skillet over medium-high heat. Toast the ancho and guajillo chiles for about 30 seconds per side, until fragrant and slightly puffed. Do not let them burn. Transfer them to a bowl, cover with boiling water, and soak for 20 minutes until softened.
2. In the same dry skillet over high heat, char the tomatoes, onion, and unpeeled garlic until blackened in spots, turning occasionally, about 8 to 10 minutes. Peel the garlic once it is cool enough to handle.
3. Add the cumin seeds to the dry skillet and toast for about 60 seconds, until fragrant.
4. Drain the soaked chiles. Add them to a blender with the charred tomatoes, onion, and garlic, the toasted cumin, smoked paprika, cinnamon, MSG, miso paste, apple cider vinegar, dark chocolate, and vegetable broth. Blend on high until completely smooth, 1 to 2 minutes.
5. Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Carefully pour in the mole sauce and cook, stirring frequently, for 10 to 12 minutes until the sauce darkens and thickens. Taste and adjust with a little more vinegar if needed.

## Notes
The sauce gets its depth from charring, toasting, and chocolate rather than salt. If it tastes flat, add another small splash of apple cider vinegar before adding salt. Anchos are mild and slightly sweet, while guajillos have a bit more heat.
